01 The Problem

Complex yachtsdon't have to be complicated.

Modern yachts are extraordinary machines — combining advanced engineering, technical systems, and specialist equipment. But when something goes wrong, tracing the problem is hard. Information is scattered across PDFs, manuals, emails, and crew memory — with no clear path from fault to fix.

When crew change or ownership transitions, that knowledge disappears entirely. There is no structured way to manage what a vessel knows about itself.

For one of the most complex private assets in the world, this is a major gap.

Information is scattered across PDFs, manuals, and crew experience

Knowledge is lost between crew changes and ownership transitions

Fault tracing is slow — no clear path from symptom to system to fix

There is no structured way to manage what a vessel knows about itself

04 How It Works

From build to resale.One continuous record.

01

Builder structures the vessel digitally

The builder creates a vessel model — structuring it by systems, sub-systems, and equipment. Existing manuals are uploaded and all systems information is stored so every piece can be amended depending on what is installed on each vessel.

02

Systems and layouts are mapped visually

The platform creates an interactive, navigable layout of the vessel. Every system, component, and procedure is connected to the visual structure — making knowledge easy to find and understand.

03

Knowledge is stored in structured formats

Each component carries its full information stack: overview, how-to guides, maintenance schedules, troubleshooting, parts references, service history, and crew notes — all in one place.

04

Owners and crew build on it over time

The platform grows with the vessel. Crew log faults and fixes, owners track history, builders stay connected. Every entry enriches the record — through crew changes, refits, and ownership transitions.
